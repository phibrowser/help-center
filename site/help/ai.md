# Meet Phi AI

Phi's AI is built into the browser and on by default — you do not set up a separate AI account, it is included. Rather than one vague "AI", it is four parts that each do something distinct: **Memory**, the **Assistant**, **Agentic** capabilities, and **Skills**. It helps to think of them separately.

This guide covers Memory, the Assistant, and Skills. For agentic tasks and using Phi from your phone, see [Automation & Phi Link](/help/automation). Prefer a plain browser? You can turn AI off — see [Privacy & your data](/help/privacy#turning-ai-off).

## Memory

Memory is how Phi gets to know you. As you browse, it gradually builds a picture of what matters to you, what you return to, and the kinds of tasks and topics that keep showing up.

You do not teach it anything — Memory is automatic and works in the background, so you are not doing unpaid data entry for your own browser. It is stored locally on your device, and it is not a black box: you can view, manage, and delete what Phi remembers. Your memory is never used to train AI models. See [Privacy & your data](/help/privacy) for the specifics.

## Assistant

The Assistant is the companion you talk to inside the browser. During onboarding you choose its name and avatar, so it has its own identity instead of feeling like a generic chatbot.

There are a few ways to reach it:

- Use the **floating chat button**.
- **Right-click** a page, a link, or selected text and ask the assistant about it directly.

Asking in context beats copying things into a separate chat window. The Assistant can answer questions, summarize content, explain pages, and help with tasks, drawing on context from the current tab, your Phi Memory, and your connected external data. Because it can see a whole Split View or Tab Group, you can ask across related pages without explaining your setup.

## Skills

Skills are reusable workflows. Teach the assistant a multi-step task once, and it can repeat it later on demand — turning a recurring job into a single request.
