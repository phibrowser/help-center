# Meet your assistant

Phi comes with an assistant built into the browser and on by default — you do not set up a separate AI account or sign in anywhere, it is simply there. One of the first things you do is give it a name and an avatar, and from then on you just call it by that name.

That naming is the point: rather than thinking of it as "an AI", a chatbot, or a model you operate, it is meant to feel like someone who works alongside you in the browser — a presence with its own identity that gets to know you over time. The rest of this guide describes the different sides of that companion:

- **Memory** — how it gets to know you.
- **Talking to it** — how you reach it and ask for things.
- **Skills** — workflows it can carry out for you.
- **Acting on your behalf** — it can take actions and run tasks, not just answer. See [Automation & Phi Link](/help/automation).

Prefer a plain browser with none of this? You can turn it all off — see [Privacy & your data](/help/privacy#turning-ai-off).

## Memory

Memory is how it gets to know you. As you browse, it gradually builds a picture of what matters to you, what you return to, and the kinds of tasks and topics that keep showing up.

You do not teach it anything — Memory is automatic and works in the background, so you are not doing unpaid data entry for your own browser. What it remembers is stored locally on your device, and it is not a black box: you can view, manage, and delete any of it. It is never used to train AI models. See [Privacy & your data](/help/privacy) for the specifics.

## Talking to it

Because you named it during onboarding, it has its own identity rather than feeling like a generic chatbot — you talk to it by name.

There are a few ways to reach it:

- Use the **floating chat button**.
- **Right-click** a page, a link, or selected text and ask about it directly.

Asking in context beats copying things into a separate chat window. It can answer questions, summarize content, explain pages, and help with tasks, drawing on context from the current tab, what it remembers about you, and your connected external data. Because it can see a whole Split View or Tab Group, you can ask across related pages without explaining your setup.

## Skills

Skills are reusable workflows. Teach it a multi-step task once, and it can repeat it later on demand — turning a recurring job into a single request.
