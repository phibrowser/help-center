# Meet your assistant

Phi comes with an assistant built into the browser and on by default — you do not set up a separate AI account or sign in anywhere, it is simply there. One of the first things you do is give it a name and an avatar, and from then on you just call it by that name.

That naming is the point: rather than thinking of it as "an AI", a chatbot, or a model you operate, it is meant to feel like someone who works alongside you in the browser — a presence with its own identity that gets to know you over time. The rest of this guide describes the different sides of that companion:

- **[Memory](/guides/memory/)** — how it gets to know you.
- **Talking to it** — how you reach it and ask for things.
- **Chat with Tabs** — bring open pages into the conversation as context.
- **[Skills](/guides/skills/)** — workflows it can carry out for you.
- **Acting on your behalf** — it can take actions and run tasks, not just answer. See [Automation & Phi Link](/guides/automation/).

Prefer a plain browser with none of this? You can turn it all off — see [Privacy & your data](/guides/privacy/#turning-ai-off).

## Memory

Memory is how it gets to know you. As you browse, it quietly builds a picture of what matters to you — automatically, stored locally on your device, and yours to view, manage, and delete. See [Memory](/guides/memory/) for the full picture.

## Talking to it

Because you named it during onboarding, it has its own identity rather than feeling like a generic chatbot — you talk to it by name.

There are a few ways to reach it:

- Click the **Chat** button. Where it sits depends on your [layout](/guides/layouts/): the bottom of the sidebar in Performance, or the top-right of the page header in Balanced and Comfortable.
- **Right-click** a page, a link, or selected text and ask about it directly.

Asking in context beats copying things into a separate chat window. It can answer questions, summarize content, explain pages, and help with tasks, drawing on context from the current tab, what it remembers about you, and your connected external data.

## Chat with Tabs

The assistant can bring the pages you have open into a conversation as context. Attach one or more tabs and ask across them — compare them, summarize them, or pull details from several at once — without copying anything into a separate window. Because it understands [Split View and Tab Groups](/guides/layouts/), attaching a grouped tab can bring the whole set along, so you can ask about related pages without explaining how they fit together.

By default, opening the assistant attaches the tab you are currently on, so you can ask about what is in front of you right away. If you would rather start each conversation with nothing attached, turn off **Automatically add current tab as context to new conversation** under **Settings → Phi AI → AI Sidebar**. You can still attach tabs by hand whenever you want.

## Skills

Skills are reusable workflows. Teach it a multi-step task once, and it can repeat it later on demand — turning a recurring job into a single request. Phi ships a set of built-in Skills you can browse and add, and you can create your own. See [Skills](/guides/skills/) for the full picture.
